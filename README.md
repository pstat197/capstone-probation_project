#  San Luis Obispo Probation Project

##  The SLO Probation Department is trying to determine the following:

* Risk - How much supervision & treatment resources should be prioritized for high risk individuals?
* Need - What interventions are needed against criminal behaviors?
* Responsivity - How should those treatment resources be adjusted personally when based on the risks and needs 
  of the individuals in probation?

## Background

* SLO County Probation uses risk/need assessment tools to support supervision and release decisions
* Tool performance can differ locally due to differences in:
    * Client population & base rates
    * Policies/practices (supervision intensity, diversion, services)
    * Outcome definitions and data systems
* Local validation checks whether scores accurately predict outcomes in SLO County and supports transparent reporting 

## Tools & Decisions

* _LS/CMI_: adult probation - overall risk/need level
* _PSA_: pretrial - risk of reoffense, violent reoffense, failure to appear
* _Static-99_: sexual reoffense risk (used with LS/CMI)
* _ODARA_: domestic violence reoffense risk (used with LS/CMI)

## Objectives

* (1) Validate whether probation assessment tools predict local outcomes in SLO County

* (2) Compare tool scores to outcomes (e.g., reoffense, violent reoffense, FTA)

* (3) Summarize predictive accuracy
